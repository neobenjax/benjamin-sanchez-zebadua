## The Mission
The CN Cycle for CHEO is a premier fundraising event dedicated to supporting pediatric oncology. With over 6,000 cyclists and participants, the event requires flawless logistical execution and rigorous safety protocols across Ottawa's urban core.

## The Role
As a **Route Captain**, I managed a designated high-traffic segment of the event. My primary responsibilities included:
- **Team Leadership:** Providing direct guidance and support to Route Assistants.
- **Logistical Coordination:** Liaising with event officials and local authorities to ensure route integrity.
- **Safety Management:** Overseeing barricade deployment, crowd control, and emergency path clearance for thousands of athletes and families.

## Architect's Lessons

**Leadership through Involvement**: In software architecture, the best technical leads are those who understand the code at the ground level while holding the big picture. Volunteering as a Route Captain reinforced this philosophy. It wasn't about standing back and delegating; it was about working alongside the crew—actively moving barricades and accompanying volunteers through long, demanding shifts.

By looking for the immediate needs of the team—providing water, clear timelines, and real-time event updates—I ensured they had the stability to perform. I learned that encouraging volunteers is the catalyst for participant success; when the crew feels supported and informed, they have the energy to cheer on and inspire every one of the 6,000+ participants. By maintaining situational awareness and empowering the team with clear directives, we created a self-regulating system that ensured the safety and victory of the entire community.
